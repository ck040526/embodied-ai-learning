# Embodied AI Learning Journey

This repository records my learning process in Embodied AI, robotics, SLAM, ROS, and robot control.

## About This Repository

I created this repository to build a structured learning portfolio for Embodied AI.

Embodied AI is an important direction that combines artificial intelligence, robotics, perception, control, and interaction with the physical world. Unlike traditional AI systems that mainly process text, images, or data, Embodied AI focuses on intelligent agents that can perceive the environment, make decisions, and take actions in real or simulated environments.

This repository is used to document my learning notes, reading summaries, technical concepts, and practice projects related to Embodied AI.

## Why I Am Learning Embodied AI

My academic background is in Electrical and Electronic Engineering, and I am interested in the connection between AI, robotics, intelligent control, and real-world engineering systems.

Through this learning process, I hope to understand:

* How robots perceive the environment
* How robots localize themselves and build maps
* How robots plan actions and make decisions
* How control systems help robots move safely and accurately
* How AI models can be connected with physical robotic systems
* How Embodied AI can be applied in future intelligent machines

### 3. SLAM

SLAM stands for Simultaneous Localization and Mapping.

It allows a robot to build a map of an unknown environment while also estimating its own position inside that map.

Topics include:

* Localization
* Mapping
* Odometry
* Visual SLAM
* LiDAR SLAM
* Visual-Inertial Odometry
* Loop closure
* Sensor fusion

### 4. Robot Perception

## My Current Understanding

Embodied AI is not only about making an AI model more intelligent. It is about connecting AI with a body, sensors, actions, and the physical world.

A simple way to understand it is:

```text
Embodied AI = Artificial Intelligence + Physical Body + Environment Interaction
```

SLAM is one of the foundations of Embodied AI because a robot needs to know where it is and understand the environment before it can move safely and complete tasks.

A simple way to understand SLAM is:

```text
SLAM = Localization + Mapping
```

In other words, SLAM allows a robot to answer two important questions:

```text
Where am I?
What does the environment look like?
```
